Strong project pages should answer three questions quickly: what problem was solved, what evidence shows the solution worked, and what decisions reveal engineering or research judgment.

For my own portfolio, I want every project write-up to include the problem, my role, technical approach, measurable results, and what I would improve next. That structure helps recruiters evaluate execution and helps professors see research potential.

The next step is to replace placeholder examples with real post-project notes from completed coursework, experiments, and collaborations.
